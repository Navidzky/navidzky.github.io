Research

When Outside Options Shape the Labor Market: A Directed Search Perspective
Working Paper
This paper studies how differences in outside options across workers affect wages, firm entry, and job allocation in the labor market. I develop a directed search model with two types of workers—skilled and unskilled—and two types of firms—high-tech and low-tech. Only skilled workers have outside options, creating an asymmetry between worker groups. The results show that stronger outside options raise posted wages for skilled workers but can reduce high-tech firm entry, potentially lowering overall wage opportunities in the market.
The Heterogeneous Effects of Exogenous Shocks on Public Transportation Demand
Working Paper  ·  With Kowsar Yousefi and Amir Samimi
This study examines how sudden gas price increases and the COVID-19 pandemic affected subway ridership. A gas price shock raised subway usage by approximately 3.9%, yielding a cross-price elasticity of about 0.044. In contrast, the COVID-19 pandemic reduced subway usage by approximately 42.6%.
Modeling the Accuracy of Traffic Crash Prediction Models (2022)
Journal Publication  ·  IATSS Research  ·  With Mohammad Hesam Rashidi, Soheil Keshavarz, Parham Pazari, and Amir Samimi
This study models the forecasting accuracy of traffic crash prediction across 31 provinces of Iran. Results show that crash variability, average historical crash count, employment ratio, GDP per capita, and traffic exposure all affect forecasting accuracy.
